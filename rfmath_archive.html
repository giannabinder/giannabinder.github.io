RFMath Library: Computational Electromagnetics

RFMath is a high-performance, object-oriented Python library designed for the rapid analysis of cascaded microwave networks and active devices. By leveraging NumPy-vectorized operations, the engine computes complex S-parameter responses across wide frequency sweeps without the overhead of traditional EDA GUI environments.

Modular Class Architecture
  • RFNetworks (Core Analysis): The backbone of the engine. It generates frequency-dependent transfer matrices for lumped/distributed elements, performs vectorized ABCD-to-S-parameter transformations, and utilizes optimized matrix multiplication to cascade arbitrary $N$-port networks.
  • RFFilters (Filter Synthesis): Automates the mathematical synthesis of $N$-order Chebyshev bandpass filters. It calculates exact low-pass prototype values mathematically (bypassing lookup tables) and dynamically scales them into physical LC and distributed Admittance Inverter ($J$) topologies.
  • RFStability (Active Device Analysis): Evaluates transistor parameters for high-frequency amplifier design. It calculates Rollett’s stability factor ($K$), $\Delta$, and generates the exact geometric centers and radii for source and load stability circles.
  • RFMatching (Impedance & Gain): Computes simultaneous conjugate match conditions and maximum theoretical transducer gains ($G_{Tmax}$). It also features an analytical geometry solver to calculate exact wavelength measurements for single-stub transmission line matching networks.
